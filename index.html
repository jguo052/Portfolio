Classifying Sounds

I trained a convolutional neural network to classify short audio files as either a cough, laugh, sigh, sneeze, sniff, or throat clear. This model was trained on Mel-Frequency Cepstral Coefficients (MFCCs) data extracted from roughly 21,000 short audio files. You can test this model by creating and feeding in a six second recording in my Streamlit application deployed here.

Reddit Natural Language Processing

I created a classification model to determine whether a reddit post came from r/Seattle or r/SeattleWA. To train these models I collected roughly five thousand reddit posts using Pushshift's API. The posts were then cleaned, the stop-words were removed, and the words were stemmed.

Housing Prices

I built a linear regression model to predict housing prices in Ames, Iowa. After checking the line assumptions and using VIF scores to decrease multicollinearity, I performed inference on the coefficients of the regression model.
